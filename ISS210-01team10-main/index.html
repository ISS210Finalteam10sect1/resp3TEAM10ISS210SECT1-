About Stereotyping

According to MyJove Corporation, the definiton of a stereotype is the specific belief or assumption about individuals based solely on their membership in a particular group.
This can include things like microagressions or biases that are unintentionally misrepresenting a certain group.
Stereotypes tend to be negative which leads to prejudice, a negative attitude or feeling to the group being stereotyped.
Prejudice can lead to an individual doing harmful actions to those are stereotypicalized.
This is known as discrimination, which is the negative actions towards an individual being stereotyped.
Stereotypes, prejudice, and discrimination all come from the origin of social learning as one learns how to act upon social norms.
This can include the exmaple of children or training individuals learning prejudiced beliefs from their parents, teachers, or those in Supreme Power.
The usage of mainstream media like the news or social media also plays a huge role in impacting the way people perceive stereotypes.

Stereotypes about overweight students and their impact on grading among physical education teachers


Many students perform poorly in sports because they are overweight.
Physical education teachers think that these overweight students need more attention and care.
They often avoid physical education classes and are teased by those around them.

Overweight individuals are stereotyped as lazy, stupid, weak and incompetent.
Many people think they are obese because of a lack of self-control, which also imposes a burden on society.
These teachers also had lower expectations for overweight students than for normal-weight students and had lower expectations than those who taught subjects not related to exercise or health.
In such cases, stereotypes have a strong influence on students.
Those students may feel that they are discriminated against, resulting in a weariness of the heart, not willing to go to school, and do not want to communicate with others, so that they will slowly away from society, even depression.
Thus, stereotypes can lead to bias in judgment.
However, their influence can be mitigated by people's motivation to control their biases.
People who are more prejudiced agree with stronger stereotypes than people who are less prejudiced.
The need to respond without prejudice can be extrinsic or intrinsic motivation.
Experimental studies have shown that overweight students achieve better grades than normal-weight children.
It is an important task for teachers to help all children enjoy physical activity.
However, one of the basic prerequisites for the successful completion of this task is that teachers must successfully overcome their own stereotypes and discriminatory behavior.

Economic inequality shapes social class stereotyping

Inequality causes the rich and the poor to be stereotyped differently and generally more negatively.
Such types of stereotypes matter as they have the potential to collectively shape motivations to reduce or maintain inequality.
Research on social class stereotypes has mainly focused on the Vertical dimension.
Much like the name suggests in vertical stereotyping the wealthier groups have higher social status, and thus are viewed as more competent and ambitious than the middle-income groups, and the poorer groups who are considered of lower social status and are viewed as lazy and incompetent.
Along these lines, many societies stereotype the wealthy as cold and immoral, as they may be perceived as acquiring their wealth through illegitimate and selfish means. However, the poor are generally perceived as friendlier and more moral than the wealthy, even though this is not always the case.
It’s usually the poor that suffers through the negative stereotypes, but in some cases even the rich can get affected due the mistrust between the two social classes. For example, the wealthy become fearful of falling while the poor experience enhanced relative deprivation, this can lead to conflicts between the two groups.
Since the rich is generally associated with assertive, smart, and hard-working people, its usually the rich who frames the perceptions of society (the makers), while the poor are stereotyped as lazy, not hardworking, and incompetent (the losers).
Negatively stereotyping affects both these social groups,it creates toxicity and intergroup prejudices.Intergroup relations between the wealthy and poor tend to be more negative and competitive in unequal societies.
To the extent that it influences the stereotype content of both the social class categories, thus making both the wealthy and poor groups appear unfriendly and immoral.
It leads to intergroup hostility and toxic prejudices.
